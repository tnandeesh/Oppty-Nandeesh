Quantum Physics and Special Relativity

In this final chapter we propose to see how special relativity is taken into account in quantum physics. We do not address general relativity, spacetime curvature as a manifestation of gravitation, as this is currently a separate and incompatible theory, though both general relativity and quantum physics are extremely efficient in their own field. We have already mentioned some effects that special relativity has in quantum physics. The Schrödinger–Klein–Gordon Equation

The Schrödinger–Klein–Gordon equation concerns only spinless physical objects, like the meson, and, by extension, any situation where the spin does not play an important role. This is the case, for instance, in electronic microscopy: the spin of the incident fast electrons can be ignored provided that the magnetic interaction with the sample investigated is negligible or null.
